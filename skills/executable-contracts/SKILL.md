# Executable Contracts (v2.0)

## Overview
A contract is real when a machine on the OTHER side of the seam exercises it. Prose contracts converge on
contradiction; fixture-bearing contracts converge on integration.

## When to use (and NOT)
- Use when: any cross-repo seam is about to be designed, versioned, or changed.
- **NOT** for: intra-repo module boundaries (normal types/tests) or auditing a peer's current state
  (→ `cross-system-reality-audit`).

## Process
1. **One contract per seam, in the PRODUCER's repo** (`templates/api-contract.md`) — frozen BEFORE the other
   side designs. Parallel design is the failure mode this artifact exists to prevent — and the recorded
   incident is that exact sentence appearing in a contract created in parallel with its contradicting twin.
2. **Fixtures ship with it** [A]: real request/response examples a machine can replay, generated by a script,
   living next to the contract.
3. **Consumer-CI heartbeat** [P+A]: the consumer's CI replays the fixtures; that job is the contract's pulse.
   No consumer CI ⇒ the contract is presumed dead and may not gate anything.
4. **"Peer constraints overridden" — mandatory section** [P]: every locked pin / standing ruling of the
   consuming repo that this contract overrides, each citing the consumer document and the authorizing
   signature. **A contract/ECR citing no document from the consuming repo is rejected on form.**
5. **Credentials at spec time** [P+A]: tokens/scopes a consumer needs are named in the contract and requested
   day one (founder acts are the long pole).
6. **Production-over-localhost wiring (N7)** [A]: cross-system config points at the PRODUCTION seam by
   default — local peer processes drift (stale secrets, old code) and produce false integration failures.
7. **Consumers never re-implement producer logic** [A]: a consumer needing new logic means a new producer-side
   projection (one-derivation-many-consumers).

## Red flags
- Two documents claiming to freeze the same seam (the dual-contract incident — stop and reconcile via ECR).
- A "frozen" contract whose fixtures nothing replays.
- A ratification that pre-decides an open founder-gated question without an explicit signature (the recorded
  incident: two such questions silently pre-decided).
- Integration claims arriving as relayed prose — contracts + probes, never narrative (doctrine D-CONTRACT).

## Verification (of this skill's own output)
- The consumer's CI contract-test job exists and is green against the shipped fixtures.
- The "peer constraints overridden" section cites consumer documents (or an explicit "none").
- One `grep` for the seam name across both repos finds exactly ONE contract home.
