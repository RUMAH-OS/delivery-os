**Recurring signal (corroborates the charter root):** every entry except FV-3 is a *workflow / cross-system* failure that survived *component* QA — discovered at the founder's live validation, the latest/most-expensive point. FV-3 is the deployment-ownership gap. **LC-1 sharpens the root one level deeper:** even *with* the executable seam contract + seam-gate (#1/#2) in place, the gate proves every *component* (event) correct yet cannot prove the *lifecycle* correct — a component-correct system can still be workflow-incorrect when an entire transition is simply absent. The founder's framing: *"A founder does not think in events. A founder thinks in workflows and business reality — the contract is active, terminated, reinstated; the system must stay correct across the whole lifecycle, not only at individual event boundaries."* Three co-equal v6 targets: **component-quality** (seam-gate), **lifecycle/workflow-quality** (#16), AND **founder-burden** — business workflows drive the architecture, not the reverse.
